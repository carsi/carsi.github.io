Work

I currently design large scale interactive touchscreen experiences at LKC Creative in Raleigh, North Carolina. Here are a few highlights:

  • Duke Energy History

    Purpose: To recognize legacy companies and their shared history within the context of American history and share current stories the highlight the work of Duke Energy employees.

    News Coverage about the exhibit

    I worked on the conception and design of the interactive touchscreen wall in the lobby of one of the Duke Energy corporate headquarters buildings in Charlotte, NC.

    Through initial research and conversations with employees, we found that several of the current Duke Energy employees came from acquisitions and mergers. Furthermore, older employees who are retiring in the next few years will be replaced by a younger population with no knowledge of this company history.

    The interactive wall is part of an internal exhibit that highlights 180 years of Duke Energy and legacy company history, serving to educate and reflect upon the events of the past. The interactive wall includes a shared timeline, company trivia, and current employee highlights.

    Role: Design strategy, user experience, and user interface design

  • Ulysses S. Grant Presidential Library

    Working with historians at Mississippi State University, I designed a series of 6 interactives that let visitors learn about the character of President Ulysses S. Grant and draw their own conclusions of the Union general and president, in the context of the south.

    News Coverage about the Library

    Role: Design strategy, user experience, and user interface design

  • NC State Athletics Walk of Fame and History

    A grand hall with five interactive screens that tell the incredible stories of past accolades and hall of fame inductees, the 1974/1983 basketball championship teams, legendary basketball coaches Kay Yow and Jim Valvano, and the current athletics program. The media is part of the NC State Athletics Walk of Fame and History at Reynolds Coliseum in Raleigh, North Carolina and is open to the public.
    NC State Athletics Hall of Fame Project on LKC

    Role: User experience and user interface design

    Hall of Fame Interactive

    A media piece that recognizes Hall of Fame Inductees and tells their stories through videos, imagery, and content. Visitors can search for inductees through or discover new honorees through browsing.

    Wolfpack Wall

    A multi-screen interactive wall with a variety of media, including videos, trivia, social media, athlete highlights, and Tuffy Toss (a Wolfpack themed arcade-like game).

    Conference Accolades Interactive Data Visualization

    An interactive data visualization that shows conference awards of athletes from all Wolfpack sports.

    1974 and 1984 National Champions Interactive

    NC State has won the Men’s Basketball National Championships twice in its athletic history: once in 1974 with head coach Everett Case and again in 1983 with head coach Jim Valvano. This interactive shows the path to both success stories.

    Jim Valvano and Kay Yow Legacy Interactive

    If you have ever heard of NC State’s athletics, you have probably heard of legendary basketball coaches Jim Valvano and Kay Yow. Both coaches are well respected not only for their athletic achievements but for their impact on the NC State community. Their respective stories are reflected in this interactive. Their memories live on in their speeches and foundations to fund cancer research.


Process

I use design thinking methods and storytelling to make a compelling case for users. Working closely with project managers and content experts, I am able to leverage their knowledge to co-create interactive experiences that serve the everyday person.

Steps in my Design Process

  1. Initial research on the subject matter and users
  2. Design strategy to align with the vision of the organization
  3. User experience design to determine user needs and motivations
  4. Ideation, including design methods and sketching wireframes
  5. High fidelity user interface design
  6. Testing through prototyping
